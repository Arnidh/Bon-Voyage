Chennai

The home of vibrant color and rich heritage

Book now


Chennai, often referred to as the "Gateway to the South," is the capital city of the Indian state of Tamil Nadu. Nestled along the Coromandel Coast on the Bay of Bengal, Chennai is a vibrant metropolis that seamlessly blends its rich cultural heritage with modernity. Here's some places to visit in Chennai:

1. Marina Beach: Marina Beach is one of the longest urban beaches in the world and a popular spot for locals and tourists alike. Enjoy a leisurely walk along the sandy shores and take in the view of the Bay of Bengal.



2. Fort St. George: Built by the British in 1644, Fort St. George is the first English fortress in India. It houses the St. Mary's Church, the oldest Anglican church in India, and the Fort Museum



3. Guindy National Park: Located in the heart of the city, Guindy National Park is a protected area with diverse flora and fauna. It's a great place for nature lovers and birdwatchers.



4. Vivekananda House (Ice House): This historic house is where Swami Vivekananda stayed for a brief period. It now serves as a museum and cultural center dedicated to the life and teachings of Swami Vivekananda.
