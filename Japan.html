Japan is a country rich in culture, history, and natural beauty, offering a wide range of sightseeing opportunities. Here are some popular sightseeing destinations in Japan:

1. Tokyo:
• Tokyo Tower: Iconic landmark offering panoramic views of the city.
• Shibuya Crossing: Famous pedestrian crossing in the heart of Shibuya.
• Meiji Shrine: A Shinto shrine surrounded by lush forest in Shibuya.



2. Hiroshima:
• Hiroshima Peace Memorial Park: Commemorates the victims of the atomic bomb.
• Miyajima Island: Known for the iconic "floating" torii gate at Itsukushima



3. Mount Fuji: Offers a classic view of Mount Fuji



4. Kyoto:
• Kinkaku-ji (Golden Pavilion): Zen Buddhist temple covered in gold leaf.
• Fushimi Inari Taisha: Known for its thousands of torii gates


Hotels

  • Halekulani Okinawa

    Set along a white-sand beach on the East China Sea, this chic resort is 3 km from Busena Marine Park and 54 km from Naha Airport.
    Sleek rooms offer flat-screens, minifridges and sitting areas. All come with balconies and Wi-Fi; many feature sea views. Club-level rooms have private lounge access with free breakfast, drinks and snacks. Ranging from suites to 2-bedroom villas, the upgraded quarters add living space; some have private pools or hot tubs. Room service is provided 24/7.
    Multiple dining options include an airy restaurant, an acclaimed French eatery and a trendy bar. There’s also a spa, an outdoor pool and a gym. Parking is available.

  • Sanga Ryokan 山河旅館

    This genteel hot spring inn on the banks of a river is 14 km from Kujūhana Park and 15 km from Sujiyu Hot Spring.
    Featuring tatami floors, futons and traditional short-legged tables, the chic rooms have satellite TV and minifridges, along with stone soaking tubs, and tea and coffeemaking facilities. Upgraded rooms add open-air, deep soaking tubs.
    Parking is free. There are indoor and outdoor, communal and private hot spring baths (gender-segregated) set within landscaped gardens. Kaiseki meals are available.
