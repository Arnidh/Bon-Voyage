Kolkata

The city of joy

Book now


Kolkata, the capital city of the Indian state of West Bengal, is a city rich in history, culture, and literature. The city has played a crucial role in India's colonial past and has been a center for art, literature, and social movements. Here are some of the prominent sightseeing attractions in Kolkata:

1. Victoria Memorial: A stunning marble building, the Victoria Memorial is an iconic symbol of Kolkata. It is a museum dedicated to Queen Victoria and the British colonial era, housing a vast collection of artifacts, paintings, and sculptures.



2. Howrah Bridge: One of the most recognizable landmarks of Kolkata, the Howrah Bridge is a cantilever bridge that spans the Hooghly River. It offers spectacular views, especially during the evening when it is beautifully illuminated.



3. Mother House (Missionaries of Charity): The Mother House is the headquarters of the Missionaries of Charity, founded by Mother Teresa. Visitors can pay their respects to her tomb and learn about her life and work.



4. Belur Math: The headquarters of the Ramakrishna Math and Mission, Belur Math is a serene and spiritually significant place. It was founded by Swami Vivekananda and is known for its architectural beauty and religious harmony
